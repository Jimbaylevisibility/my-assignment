Analysis of the peace treaties that concluded the WWI

According to VN Khanna in his book International Relations, The first world war which began on the 28th June 1914 has been regarded as one of the most devastating war that plunged the world. This war had been connoted as a war to end all wars but little was known that it set the stage for all other wars. The war was fought between two opposing camps, the Triple Alliance and Triple Entente. With Germany, Austria-Hungary Bulgaria and the Ottoman Empire on one side(Triple Alliance) and Britain, France, USA, Japan and Italy on the other (Triple Entente). The War ended with the unconditional surrender of Germary on the 28th July 1918. The treaties which concluded the wars were: The Peace treaty of Versailles signed with Germany, The treaty of saint Germain was signed with Austria, the Treaty of Neuilly was signed with Bulgaria and Treaty of Trianon with Hungary and the Treaty of Sevres with Ottoman empire but later the treaty of Lausaine with Turkey which became the offshot of the fallen empire of Ottoman.
